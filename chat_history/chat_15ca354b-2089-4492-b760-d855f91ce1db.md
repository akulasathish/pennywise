1. **Initiate Payment Session (PMS ➔ PennyWise)**:
   * When a customer clicks "Upgrade/Subscribe" on your PMS, your backend makes a backend `POST` request to PennyWise (`/api/payment-requests`) with the user's details.
   * PennyWise dynamically allocates the unique penny-wise amount (e.g., `Rs. 100.02` instead of `100.00`) and returns a session object.

2. **Embed or Redirect (PMS ➔ Customer)**:
   * Your PMS can redirect the landlord to the PennyWise checkout URL: `http://<pennywise-domain>/checkout.html?id=pay_xxxx`
   * Or, you can seamlessly embed the portal in an `<iframe>` within your PMS dashboard so the landlord never has to leave your site.

3. **Reconciliation Callback (PennyWise ➔ PMS)**:
   * Once PennyWise reconciles an incoming bank SMS with a pending transaction, it fires an HTTP `POST` webhook to your PMS's handler endpoint (e.g. `/api/payment-callback`).
   * Your PMS receives this callback, verifies the secret headers, and marks that user's subscription active for 30 days in your database.
